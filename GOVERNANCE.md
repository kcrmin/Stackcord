# Governance

The project is maintained by contributors with merge authority recorded in the public repository settings. Until a broader maintainer group exists, changes require one maintainer approval; security boundaries, schemas, contracts, release gates, and governance changes require two independent approvals when two eligible maintainers are available.

Decisions prefer documented technical evidence, user impact, framework neutrality, backward compatibility, and sustainable maintenance. Major product changes use a proposal linked to stable design IDs and keep dissenting alternatives in the decision record. Maintainers with a conflict of interest disclose it and abstain where appropriate.

Maintainers may be added after sustained, constructive contributions and demonstrated care for security and compatibility. Removal may follow inactivity, repeated policy violations, or loss of project trust, with the reason documented unless privacy or security prevents publication.

Public releases require protected tags/environments, reproducible evidence, exact release-candidate identity, and the approval policy. No maintainer may bypass the production gate alone merely because they can push code.
